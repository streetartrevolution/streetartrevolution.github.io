Back to Black 

The “Back to Black” mural is a giant sketchbook installation inspired by Keith Haring's subway chalk drawings. This installation allows the public to immerse themselves in the artists' creative work practice.

"I was on the subway platform when ,I saw my first empty black panels. I knew I had to draw on them, so I ran up to buy chalk. I started drawing and it felt incredible!”

–Keith Haring

"The term "Street Art" is defined as 'art created in public venues outside of traditionally sanctioned art galleries'. It is derived from the hip hop and graffiti culture of the 1970's and 80's and was used to express the realities of everyday life. The art form was originally considered counter culture and was not always welcomed, as it was the antithesis to what was being seen in traditional galleries. However, non-traditional yet talented artists turned walls into canvas and spray cans into paintbrushes. While this art form has since become more mainstream and acceptable and can now actually be found in certain galleries, it still tends to 'shake up' the 'status quo'. The term 'revolution' means 'the sudden and radical change in a system or state of affairs. It is this combined dynamic that caused us to choose the name 'Street Art Revolution'. Some of the most prolific artists in South Florida will not be found in upscale galleries so to make their voices heard Our mission is to give them both a platform and an audience " -Dalhia Perryan

Curator

Caron Bowman is a multi-disciplined artist ,curator and art activist. She has a Bachelor of Arts degree in History and Master of Arts degree in ESE. Her artwork has received international recognition and has been on display at the Marc Chagall Museum,Harlem Fine Arts Show, ARCO Madrid Fair, Continuum Art Fair,Scope Art Fair, and Art Africa Fair. Rapper Kendrick Lamar in association with Creative Allies showcased her artwork in NYC at the Galapagos Art Space. Extending her art practice, she has created public art installations engagements in the South Florida region for both public and private entities including Norton Museum of Art, Art Palm Beach , The Society of the Four Arts and Heradura Tequila .

Currently, she is the curator and a founding member of Street Art Revolution a mural program and arts collective. Caron emerged as a finalist for the 2012 Beck’s Green Box augmented reality series – which featured the best emerging artists around the globe. She won the 2017 Bombay Sapphire Artisan Series and represented Miami at Scope Art Fair. In addition to other honors, she has also been profiled by The Friends of the American Latino Museum, the 2018 Los Angeles African American Heritage Guide, fashion photographer Nick Knight’s – SHOWstudio, and the Tom Joyner Foundation. 

Street Art Revolution is an artist collective out of South Florida. The goal of the program is to put a spotlight on artists by utilizing the medium of murals and installations. Our collective taps into the cultural psyche of communities creating murals and public art Installations that speak to the authentic side of life. Moreover, we are diversifying and redefining the definition of public art. This hyper-creative program partners with cultural organizations both private and public to present pioneering work across multiple creative disciplines.
